1 The World Tree Faith

Due to The World Tree's inescapable presence in Talmiar, a majority of individuals – at least in some capacity – are members of the World Tree Faith. Somewhat predictably, The World Tree Faith is based in The Sovereign City. In addition to the usual paladins and clerics, there are a nontrivial number of Druids and Dryads who hold high-ranking positions in The Faith.

For many who follow it, The World Tree faith is not the only religion that they are a part of. Giving thanks to The World Tree in addition to one of the other major gods at the dinner table is a familiar sight in many households in Talmiar.

While The World Tree Faith does not have a very strong set of morals associated with it, they believe in being thankful towards The World Tree and the Gods for the great bounty that the land provides.

2 The Gods

2.1 Ouro: The Goddess of Knowledge and Arcana

Ouro is the patron goddess of academics of all forms. All graduation ceremonies are officiated by a priest of Ouro, and major graduations (such as at the magic colleges) are officiated by high ranking bishops from the Temple in Pikehood.

Ouro devotees value diligence, and always following through with their duties. They also value rationality and the pursuit of logical solutions to their problems. They often make great mediators, and arguing individuals often head to the local Church of Ouro for consultations.

2.2 Heiron: The God of the Forge, War, and Justice

Heiron – though primarily revered as the God of the Forge – is more aptly the God of all craftsman. Most craftsmen will have their workshops consecrated regularly, if for no other reason then because many customers do not trust goods from vendors that have not been blessed. Additionally, all armies receive a blessing from Heiron before going to war.

Those who follow Heiron often have a very spartan sense of justice, and believe in an "eye for an eye" punishment system, sometimes even advocating for harsher punishments when they are the ones who did wrong.

Heiron's major temple is located in Blazesong.

2.3 Tenshal: The God of Death and the Grave

At nearly every graveyard in Talmiar, there is a Church of Tenshal. A Tenshal's priest's primary duty is to conduct last rites and officiate funerals. Tenshal's devout believe that death is the ultimate peace for an individual, and would likely be offended if resurrection magic was used in their presence. They believe that death is something planned by Tenshal, and that any effort to divert from Tenshal's plan, or any effort to depart earlier than planned, would be an insult to their God.

Tenshal does not have a dedicated temple, and is a mostly de-centralized faith, but almost every city or town has a church at its graveyard.

2.4 Ifiit: The Trickster Goddess of Twilight and Tempest

Ifiit is worshiped by her collective as the Goddess of Chaos. Her followers believe that she prevents the other Gods and Goddesses from making the world completely determenistic by playing tricks on them.
